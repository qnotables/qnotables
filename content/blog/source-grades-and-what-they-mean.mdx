## Why grade sources at all

An anonymous tip from a government official is different from a named statement in a court filing. A press release from an interested party is different from an independent investigative report. Treating them the same does the reader a disservice.

Our source grades are published openly so you can weigh the claims yourself.

## The grade scale

### A — Primary Source
Original documents, court filings, legislative records, official transcripts, on-the-record statements from named principals. The claim originates here; there is no further upstream to go.

### B — Named Independent Reporting
A named reporter at an outlet with a public corrections policy, citing named or clearly described sources. The byline carries accountability.

### C — Unnamed But Attributed
"According to three officials familiar with the matter" — common in Washington and national security reporting. Useful, but cannot be verified independently. We note the attribution explicitly.

### D — Institutional Statement
Press releases, official spokespeople, organizational communications. These represent a party's position, not a neutral account of facts. Relevant, but treated as advocacy until corroborated.

### E — Unverified
Viral claims, screenshots, social media posts, and unofficial accounts that have not yet been confirmed by a B-grade or better source. We may note these exist; we do not treat them as established.

## How grades appear in coverage

Every story filed on The Wire carries a source grade in its metadata. When a story relies on multiple grades, the lowest grade is displayed — we do not average up.

A story with three A-grade sources and one C-grade source is labeled C until the C-grade claim is corroborated or removed.

## The correction obligation

When a source grade is upgraded or downgraded after publication — because a document becomes public, a source goes on record, or a claim is retracted — the story is updated and the change is logged in the corrections record.

We do not memory-hole errors. We do not silently swap one grade for another. The history stays visible.

## A note on speed

The practical consequence of this system is that we sometimes publish a story later than outlets that do not apply these standards. We consider that an acceptable cost. A well-graded C is more useful than a confidently wrong A.
